Statistics



2009

In 2009, Lionel Messi had one of the greatest seasons in football history. With FC Barcelona under coach Pep Guardiola, he helped the team achieve the historic "Sextuple" — winning six major trophies in a single year: La Liga, Copa del Rey, UEFA Champions League, Spanish Super Cup, UEFA Super Cup, and FIFA Club World Cup. Messi was the top scorer of the Champions League with nine goals and scored in the final against Manchester United. His dribbling, vision, and decisive goals made him the key figure of Barcelona’s success. At the end of the year, Messi won his first Ballon d’Or, confirming his rise as the best player in the world.

2012

In the 2011-12 season, Lionel Messi had the greatest individual campaign in football history. He scored a record-breaking 73 goals and provided 29 assists in 60 matches for Barcelona. In La Liga, he scored 50 goals, setting an all-time record. With his incredible dribbling, playmaking, and finishing, Messi was unstoppable. Although Barcelona did not win the Champions League or La Liga that year, Messi lifted four trophies: the Copa del Rey, Spanish Super Cup, UEFA Super Cup, and FIFA Club World Cup. His 2012 season confirmed him as the most dominant player in world football.

2015

In the 2014-15 season, Lionel Messi returned to his very best and led Barcelona to an incredible treble, winning La Liga, Copa del Rey, and the UEFA Champions League. He played 57 matches, scoring 58 goals and providing 27 assists across all competitions. In La Liga, he netted 43 goals and became the league’s top playmaker with 18 assists. In the Champions League, he scored 10 goals, including a legendary performance against Bayern Munich in the semifinal. Alongside Neymar and Suárez, Messi formed the famous "MSN" trio, scoring 122 goals together that season. His dribbling, creativity, and decisive goals made him the key figure of Barcelona’s success, and he was once again regarded as the best player in the world.

2019

In the 2018-19 season, Lionel Messi had another extraordinary year with Barcelona. He played 50 matches, scoring 51 goals and providing 22 assists. In La Liga, he scored 36 goals and gave 13 assists, winning the Pichichi Trophy as top scorer and also finishing as the top assist provider. In the Champions League, he scored 12 goals, including two brilliant strikes against Liverpool in the semifinal. He helped Barcelona win La Liga and the Spanish Super Cup, but the team fell short in the Copa del Rey final and was eliminated in the Champions League semifinal. Individually, Messi won the 2019 Ballon d’Or, his sixth, cementing his status as the best player in the world.

2022

In the 2021-22 season, Lionel Messi joined Paris Saint-Germain after leaving Barcelona. He played 34 matches, scored 11 goals, and provided 15 assists in all competitions, helping PSG win the French Ligue 1 title. In the Champions League, he scored 5 goals, including a stunning brace against Manchester City.On the international stage, Messi led Argentina to victory in the 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar. He scored 7 goals and provided 3 assists, winning the Golden Ball as the tournament’s best player. Argentina defeated France in an epic final that ended 3-3 after extra time and was decided by penalties. Messi became the first player in history to win both the World Cup and multiple Ballon d’Or awards while still being the decisive factor for his national team.
